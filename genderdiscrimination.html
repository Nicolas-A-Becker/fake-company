How Do We Handle Gender Discrimination in Workplace

Gender discrimination in the workplace takes many different forms, but in general it means that an employee or job seeker is discriminated against or treated unfairly because of their gender, gender identity or sexual orientation.

Possible situations:

  • not being hired, or being given a lower-paying position because of your gender identity
  • Being evaluated harshly because of gender identity
  • Being insulted, making the employee uncomfortable Being requested for sexual favors
  • Verbal/physical sexual harassment

Ways We Deal with Gender Discrimination :

  1. Create an equal opportunity policy states what measures a business takes to stop or prevent discrimination a policy to ensure every employee to have equal right and opportunity
  2. Educate employees about gender discrimination at workplace teaching employees about what constitutes gender inequality and sex discrimination at work, make sure employees understand what discrimination is
  3. Take immediate action to address complaints when complaints are made
  4. Keeping the workplace feel safe make sure your employees know that their voices are being heard and that they feel safe at work
